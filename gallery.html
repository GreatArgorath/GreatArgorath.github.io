Exposure
5th December 2024

This was a project for my second year of uni, I had felt a lot of my work so far on the course was quite impersonal, and wanted to create something for a cause more important to me, so created a short film with the goal of ampolifying the voices of fellow trans and nonbinary people. I interviewed various people I knew and combined them with my own experiences to create a script for the piece, I then split it into chunks and got some of them to read out those parts of the speech. I filmed myself reading the whole thing, and replaced the audio for those parts with their speech, before they combine together with my voice towards the end, fully in sync. It's intended to represent the disconnect between what people hear about trans people and our actual experiences and what we have to say, and how the more you listen the more you might be able to understand.

The music was created by my friend Ayla Brouwer, adapted from a song she had sent me not long prior that was about her experiences with gender dysphoria.

Doctor Who Title Sequences
▶

Uploaded: 27th May 2024

This one took about 6 months to put together, partially due to the complexity of it, but also just because of how busy I was around that time.

I created it primarily using Blender, it started out as an attempt at recreating the official title sequence from the 60th anniversary specials onwards, but after giving up on that because of how complex the fluid sims would need to be, I ended up with some great nebula shaders that I could repurpose into something else.

▶

Uploaded: 24th October 2023

This one came together pretty quickly, only a couple weeks of work, as the time vortex was reused from my previous sequence. I wanted to make something that fit what we knew about the episodes at the time, with elements from all the different titles from the show's history.

The most obvious connection is the similarities between the time vortex here and the 2005-2009 vortex, but it's also mixed in with the texture of the late 1970s vortex. The TARDIS movement at the beginning is also ripped straight from the 2010 title sequence, along with some movement at the end inspired by the 1996 movie titles. There's also asteroids coming towards the camera, like in the 1987 titles.

The Return of the Doctor Cover

Created using Photoshop, Blender and images from The Black Archive photo gallery and Unsplash
July 3rd 2025

This was the second story in the series, and had the same cover layout as the last, so when story details were released I went straight to work on creating another.

My initial concept for the piece was having the Doctor holding the planet in her hands, but I couldn't find the right images to composite together to make it work. Instead, I came up with the idea of the Doctor in the sky looking down on the planet below, and found two images of her to composite together that fit perfectly. A friend of mine, Arbrax, managed to get the original idea to work and created it herself.

One bit I did really like from the original cover was the armadillo man, so I wanted to try and incorporate that into the piece too. Using Blender, I made a very rough mix of the head of a free armadillo 3D scan I'd found and a model of a man, minus the head. It being in sillhoutte hides how janky this model really is pretty well, so it fits right in. I placed him walking towards the TARDIS (another model I had created) and the piece was complete.
